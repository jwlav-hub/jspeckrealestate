Know Before You Go

Top 20 Mortgage Questions & Answers

The most common questions buyers ask when applying for a mortgage — answered clearly.

Most conventional loans require a minimum score of 620, while FHA loans can go as low as 580 (or 500 with a 10% down payment). A higher score (720+) unlocks the best interest rates. Jeff can connect you with lenders who work with a range of credit profiles.
Down payment requirements vary by loan type. Conventional loans can be as low as 3%, FHA loans require 3.5%, and VA/USDA loans can be 0% for eligible buyers. Putting 20% down eliminates private mortgage insurance (PMI) and reduces your monthly payment.
Pre-qualification is an informal estimate based on self-reported information. Pre-approval is a formal review of your credit, income, and assets — resulting in a conditional commitment letter. Sellers and their agents take pre-approval far more seriously, especially in a competitive Las Vegas market.
Typically: last 2 years of tax returns and W-2s, last 30 days of pay stubs, last 2–3 months of bank statements, photo ID, and a list of current debts. Self-employed buyers will also need profit & loss statements. Having these ready speeds up the process significantly.
DTI compares your monthly debt payments to your gross monthly income. Most lenders prefer a DTI under 43%. A lower DTI means you can qualify for a larger loan or a better rate. Jeff can help you review your DTI and strategies to improve it before applying.
Closing costs typically range from 2%–5% of the loan amount and include lender fees, title insurance, escrow fees, prepaid taxes and insurance, and other charges. In Nevada, closing costs for a $500,000 home might be $10,000–$25,000. Some of these can be negotiated or rolled into the loan.
A fixed-rate mortgage locks in your interest rate for the life of the loan — predictable and ideal if you plan to stay long-term. An ARM starts at a lower rate but adjusts after an initial period (e.g., 5/1 ARM). ARMs can make sense if you plan to move or refinance within a few years.
Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) protects the lender if you default and is required when your down payment is less than 20%. It typically costs 0.3%–1.5% of the loan annually. You can avoid PMI by putting 20% down, using a piggyback loan, or requesting cancellation once you reach 20% equity.
An escrow account is managed by your lender and holds funds for property taxes and homeowner's insurance. Each month, a portion of your payment goes into escrow; the lender pays your tax and insurance bills from it. This ensures those obligations are always covered.
From application to closing typically takes 30–45 days, though it can be as fast as 21 days with a prepared buyer and responsive team. The timeline depends on the lender, the complexity of your financial situation, and how quickly documentation is submitted.
Yes. Self-employed buyers typically need 2 years of tax returns showing consistent income, along with a year-to-date profit & loss statement. Some lenders offer bank-statement loans that use average deposits rather than tax returns — useful if your write-offs reduce reported income.
The lender is required to provide an adverse action notice explaining why. Common reasons include low credit score, high DTI, insufficient income, or property issues. Many buyers successfully re-apply after improving their credit, paying down debt, or saving a larger down payment. Jeff can help you map a path forward.
No. Permanent residents (green card holders) can access the same loan programs as citizens. Non-permanent residents can still qualify for FHA loans with a valid work authorization. Foreign nationals have additional options through non-QM lenders. Jeff can connect you with the right lender for your status.
A licensed appraiser will assess the home's market value. Lenders require appraisals to confirm they're not lending more than the home is worth. If the appraisal comes in low, you'll need to renegotiate the price, pay the difference in cash, or walk away. A strong offer strategy from Jeff helps avoid this issue.
A rate lock guarantees your interest rate for a set period (typically 30–60 days) while your loan is processed. It protects you from rate increases before closing. If rates are volatile or rising, locking early makes sense. Jeff and your loan officer can advise on timing.
A home inspection is a professional evaluation of the property's condition. It's not legally required in Nevada, but it's strongly recommended. Inspections can reveal costly issues — roof, foundation, HVAC, plumbing — that can be used to renegotiate or request repairs before closing.
Title insurance protects you and your lender against ownership disputes, liens, or errors in public records related to the property. Lender's title insurance is almost always required. Owner's title insurance is optional but highly recommended — it's a one-time premium paid at closing.
Yes, with conditions. Gift funds from family members are acceptable for most loan types, but lenders require a gift letter confirming no repayment is expected. There are also paper-trail requirements showing the funds transferred from the donor's account. Let Jeff's lending team guide you through the documentation.
Homeownership can provide tax benefits including the mortgage interest deduction and property tax deduction (subject to IRS limits). Nevada has no state income tax, which is a significant advantage. Consult a CPA for personalized advice — Jeff can refer you to trusted tax professionals in the valley.
Nevada Housing Division (NHD) offers the Home Is Possible program with below-market rates and down payment assistance grants. There are also programs for teachers, first responders, and low-to-moderate income buyers. These programs can save thousands — ask Jeff if you qualify when you connect.
